Method (proxy de-risk)

  • Representation: MiniLM (all-MiniLM-L6-v2) embeddings as the "activation"; TF-IDF + random-init as comparison backends.
  • Probe: L2-logistic, 5-fold OOF AUROC, 2000× bootstrap CI.
  • Controls: random-init (A1.1, lexical triviality) and shuffled-retriever (A3.6).
  • Two corpora: naive (obvious boilerplate) vs hardened (single-token lie / natural injection).

Three findings that change the GPU plan

1 · The easy task is fake. A representation with no trained parameters scores AUROC 1.000 on naive attacks - high scores there reflect surface tokens, not deception. The random-init control is now mandatory beside every number.
2 · A frozen probe can't catch factual poisoning. Hardened PoisonedRAG sits at 0.17 ± 0.03 (10 seeds) - reproducibly below chance: the probe is actively misled. Detecting lies needs generative behaviour or internal/causal signals, not a linear probe. Injection (Greshake) stays detectable (0.83) - a different problem.
3 · "Cross-agent" adds nothing - correlationally. Combined never beat writer-only (Δ = +0.01, CIs overlap); the agents share text, so a shared feature ≠ transfer. Direction 1 must rest on the Phase-6 causal ablation.

Go-forward decision

Proceed to the GPU run with three forced changes: (a) headline on hardened attacks + attack-success labels; (b) ship a random-init Gemma control with every AUROC; (c) treat cross-agent transfer as a causal-ablation question, keeping the correlational probe as a baseline only. Separate "injection detection" from "factual-deception detection."
